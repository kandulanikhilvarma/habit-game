Privacy Policy

Effective date: [EFFECTIVE DATE] · Last updated: [DATE]

Kumo is a habit game published by [PUBLISHER NAME] ("we", "us"). This policy explains what data the app handles, where it goes, and how you remove it. It is written to match what the app actually does — not to reserve rights we do not use.

The short version

  • You can use Kumo without an email, a name, or an account you create — sign-in is anonymous.
  • We store the game data you generate (your creature, your habits, and when you complete them) so it survives across devices.
  • Health and usage data, when you connect it, is read on your device and never leaves it. Only the resulting "habit completed" events sync.
  • We do not sell your data, show ads, or use advertising trackers.
  • You can delete everything at any time — see Delete your account.

What we collect

Kumo works offline first: the same data lives on your device and syncs to the cloud when you are online.

DataWhy
An anonymous account identifier (a random ID from Firebase Authentication)To attach your game to a stable account without asking for personal details
Your creature (species, name you choose, level, evolution state)The game itself
Your habits (name, icon, category, schedule, optional reminder time)To show and track your quests
Completion events and daily summaries (which habit, the date and time, how much XP)To run streaks, evolution, and your Journey analytics

We do not collect your contacts, your location, your photos, or your device identifiers for advertising. If you choose to add an optional note or photo to a completion, it is stored with that completion and shown only to you.

Health and usage data [applies when you enable these features — not in the current build]

Kumo can verify some habits automatically from Health Connect (steps, sleep, exercise, mindfulness minutes) and from Android usage statistics (screen time, app usage). When you grant these permissions:

  • The raw health and usage data is read on your device only, to check your own habit rules.
  • Only the result — a "habit completed" event — syncs to the cloud. The underlying step count, sleep record, or app-usage figure never leaves your device.
  • You can disconnect a source at any time; the habit falls back to a one-tap manual check-in.

You grant and revoke these permissions in Android's own settings, and in Health Connect's permission screen, which links back to this policy.

Where your data is processed

We use a small number of service providers to run the app. They process data on our behalf under their own terms:

  • Google Firebase (Authentication, Firestore database, and push notifications) — stores your account and game data.
  • Vercel — hosts this website and our backend API.

Data may be stored and processed on servers operated by these providers, which may be located outside your country.

How long we keep it

We keep your game data until you delete it. When you delete your account, the data under your account identifier is removed from our database.
